# Public Meeting Announcement

## Jordan Lake Water Supply Allocations and Cape Fear Water Supply Evaluation

The Division of Water Resources has released draft recommendations for allocating the water supply pool in Jordan Lake. One-third of the conservation pool is dedicated and managed for public water supply with the remaining two-thirds reserved to augment river flows downstream. The draft recommendations are based on information submitted by local governments wishing to continue or acquire an allocation 

The updated Cape Fear – Neuse River Basins Hydrologic Model was used to analyze the ability of surface water dependent water systems in the Deep River, Haw River Cape Fear River, Neuse River, and Contentnea Creek sub-basins to meet their expected water demands through 2060. Demand projections were derived from information presented in local water supply plans and consultation with local officials. The results of this analysis is presented in the “Draft Cape Fear River Water Supply Evaluation”. 

Allocations from the water supply pool are limited to needs over 30 years. The hydrologic model was used to analyze allocation requests to meet water supply needs through 2045. The allocation analysis and recommendations are presented in the “Draft Jordan Lake Water Supply Allocation Recommendations”. 

Both documents were presented to the Environmental Management Commission at its January 2016 meeting. Copies of both documents, along with the allocation applications, staff’s presentation to the EMC and other supporting documents, are available on the Divisions’ website at : [http://deq.nc.gov/about/divisions/water-resources/planning/basin-planning/map-page/cape-fear-river-basin-landing/jordan-lake-water-supply-allocation/jordan-lake-water-supply-allocation-round-4](http://deq.nc.gov/about/divisions/water-resources/planning/basin-planning/map-page/cape-fear-river-basin-landing/jordan-lake-water-supply-allocation/jordan-lake-water-supply-allocation-round-4) .
