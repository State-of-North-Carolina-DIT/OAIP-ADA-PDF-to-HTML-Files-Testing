A draft Hazardous Waste Management Permit (permit) has been prepared for Safety-Kleen Archdale. The draft permit will allow Safety-Kleen to store hazardous waste at the facility under the Federal Resource Conservation and Recovery Act (RCRA).

North Carolina is authorized by the United States Environmental Protection Agency to administer RCRA including the Hazardous and Solid Waste Amendments (HSWA) of 1984. The State has determined that Safety-Kleen Archdale’s proposed activities, as identified in the permit application, satisfy the full intent of the North Carolina Hazardous Waste Management Rules and Solid Waste Management Act, as amended. When finalized, this draft permit issued by the State of North Carolina will constitute a complete permit under the Federal Resource Conservation and Recovery Act.

## Background

Safety-Kleen Systems, Incorporated currently operates a hazardous waste storage facility at 6182 Old Mendenhall Road, Archdale, Randolph County, North Carolina (Latitude 35.914970, Longitude – 80.015718). The facility performs collection, replacement, accumulation, and storage of spent mineral spirits, immersion cleaner, paint waste, and dry-cleaning wastes as a service to industrial maintenance, automotive repair and dry-cleaning businesses. RCRA-permitted areas include the warehouse container storage areas; the flammable waste container storage area; one 20,000-gallon aboveground storage tank; and two dumpster/drum washer units located in the Return & Fill Station.

Because of previous spills, the facility has undergone several rounds of soil and groundwater sampling and assessment starting in 1989. Impacted soil and groundwater were detected at the former tank farm (southern source area) and the former wet dumpster and former 10,000-gallon AST area (northern source area). Impacted soil was removed from the southern source area. Aggressive fluid vapor recovery (AFVR) was used to extract LNAPL. A soil vapor extraction system was installed for soil remediation. Groundwater monitoring for volatile and semivolatile organic compounds is ongoing.
