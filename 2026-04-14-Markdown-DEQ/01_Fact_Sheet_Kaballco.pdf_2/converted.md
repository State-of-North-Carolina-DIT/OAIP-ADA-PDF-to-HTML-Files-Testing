A draft Post-Closure Permit has been prepared for Kaba Ilco Corporation under the Federal Resource Conservation and Recovery Act (RCRA). North Carolina has been authorized by the United States Environmental Protection Agency to administer RCRA including the Hazardous and Solid Waste Amendments (HSWA) of 1984. The State has determined that Kaba Ilco's proposed activities as identified in the Part B Permit Renewal Application satisfies the full intent of the North Carolina Hazardous Waste Management Rules and Solid Waste Management Act as amended. When finalized, this permit issued by the State of North Carolina will constitute a complete permit under the Federal Resource Conservation and Recovery Act.

Kaba Ilco has been in operation since 1974 and produces a variety of security products, primarily keys, lock mechanisms and associated components. A former surface impoundment was operated from 1977 until 1981 and used for the storage and evaporation of electroplating wastewater. The impoundment was closed in 1986 by excavation, stabilization, and reburial of contaminated soil. A reinforced concrete was installed. Residual groundwater contamination is being remediated with a pump and treat system. Annual groundwater sampling is conducted at on- and offsite monitoring wells.

The North Carolina Hazardous Waste Management Rules require that the public be given a forty-five-day period to comment on the draft modified permit. This forty-five-day period will commence on Monday, July 17, 2023. The draft permit and all data submitted by the applicant is available as part of the administrative record at the https://deq.nc.gov/about/divisions/waste-management/laserfiche.
